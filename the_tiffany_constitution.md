### THE DISTRICT CLAUSE

#### Section 1. Right of Self-Determination



No People shall be permanently bound to the Union against their will, nor shall any District permanently bind a People to its authority. The right of self-determination shall be preserved at every level of government and may be exercised only through the peaceful and democratic procedures established by this Constitution.



#### Section 2. Recognition of a People



A distinct People numbering not less than twenty million persons may petition for self-determination. The National Management of the People's Discrepancies shall investigate such petition and determine whether the petitioning population constitutes a distinct People under the laws of the Union and satisfies all constitutional requirements for consideration.



#### Section 3. Referendum on Independence



Upon certification of a valid petition, the National Management of the People's Discrepancies shall conduct a referendum among the affected People. If a majority of votes lawfully cast favor independence, and all constitutional requirements have been satisfied, the Agency shall recognize the petitioning People as an Independent District.



#### Section 4. Constitution of an Independent District



An Independent District shall adopt a constitution consistent with the Democratic Principles of the Union. Such constitution shall provide for:

(a) representative government;

(b) periodic free and fair elections;

(c) the protection of fundamental rights;

(d) a District Clause substantially equivalent in purpose and effect to that contained within this Constitution; and

(e) lawful procedures by which the People may amend their government or seek reunification with the Union.



#### Section 5. Preservation of Democratic Government



No District constitution shall abolish, suspend, or materially impair the right of a People to self-government, self-determination, peaceful political participation, or the lawful replacement of those exercising governmental authority.



#### Section 6. Reunification

An Independent District may petition for reunification with the Union through procedures established by this Constitution and by law. No District shall be denied the opportunity to seek reunification through peaceful and democratic means.



### THE SEPARATION OF DEPARTMENTS AND AGENCIES



#### Section 1. Distinction



The institutions of the Union shall be divided between Departments and Agencies.



Departments shall be organs of Government established for the administration of public policy and the execution of laws. Departments shall be subject to the lawful direction of the elected Government.



Agencies shall be constitutional institutions established for the administration, certification, and execution of democratic and constitutional processes. Agencies shall possess only those powers expressly granted by this Constitution and shall not be subject to political direction except as provided herein.



#### Section 2. Political Neutrality



No Agency shall formulate public policy, advocate political positions, or exercise powers not expressly granted by this Constitution. Agencies shall remain politically neutral and shall faithfully administer their constitutional duties.



#### Section 3. Independence



No Department, elected official, or political body shall interfere with the lawful execution of an Agency's constitutional responsibilities.



#### Section 4. Constitutional Supremacy



Where a Departmental action conflicts with a constitutional duty of an Agency, the constitutional duty shall prevail.



#### Section 5. Establishment



The Agencies required by this Constitution shall be established and maintained at all times. No Government may abolish, suspend, or materially impair the operation of a constitutionally required Agency.
